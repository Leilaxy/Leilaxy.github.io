Memory

Time is not eternal, but love and memories are.

My aim in this project is to explore the links between cities generated by human activity and those generated by the world of code. I argue that the regularity and irregularity of the generation of urban architecture is similar to that of digital generation. Firstly I simplified the building form and then used the mouse to generate paths within the p5js software to randomly generate buildings. These are city streets that do not exist in reality, but in which people will find their own memories. This is because everyone can see the path they have taken. I wanted to express through such data generation that the city carries our memories, time passes, but memories are an important component of our humanity, because with memories we have love, time is not eternal, but love and memories are

First of all a simplification of the building form. Street formation is related to roads. The set of points at which the mouse passes is obtained, these point sets are connected to form roads, and trees are generated at the location of each point in the point set, each tree being generated using a recursive approach. The building can change its form by changing the approximate height and width of the resulting building. Based on the paths, a number of nodes are generated using vectors to move along the road, with the movement of each node forming a different trajectory, and by adjusting the relative positions of the nodes the building and the lines around the building are generated. Through the different effects and the randomly generated streets, the video changes from the blurred effect of the city in memory to the modern reality of the modern city. This is a way of evoking the memory of the city. No matter how much time has passed, we are reminded of the past when we see familiar streets and buildings.
